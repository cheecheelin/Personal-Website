Stitcher Product Challenge


The Challenge

Podcasting has gained popularity recently thanks to the rapid growth in smartphone use and the rise of wildly popular content like Serial. However, the technology that supports the creation and distribution of podcasts has evolved at a much slower pace compared to other mediums. Stitcher is one of the most widely used podcast platforms on the market right now.

Act as Stitcher's lead PM and assume listeners who interact with other users have a better overall experience. Determine:

  • The first feature to build to test this assumption and why
  • How this feature looks and behaves
  • Discuss tradeoffs, risks and metrics for success


Understanding the Challenge at Hand

To start off, I needed to clarify the parameters of this hypothesis: "Listeners who interact with other listeners have a better overall experience". I would need to operationalize the definition of "interact" and "better overall experience".

The essence of "interact" suggested any interpersonal communication between two listeners, and that could mean anything from common social media action patterns such as 'liking', 'sharing', 'commenting', 'discussing', 'reacting', 'discovering', and 'following'.

"Better overall experience" is a broad concept, and could mean anything from 'increased satisfaction with the app', 'increased retention in usage of the app', or 'increased functionality of the app'.

Now that I had determined a number of possible paths these definitions could take, I decided to delve deep into the needs of the user to thoroughly determine what feature I would build to test this hypothesis.

3. Ideating

In the process of brainstorming new features to address these user needs and goals, I came up with quite a few ideas, including the following:

  • Integrations with wearables (headphones, apple watch etc. since their hands are busy) to bookmark/highlight/annotate their favorite sections in a podcast and share with other users
  • Real time transcribing of comments (while a podcast is playing) and having 'pings' to let fellow listeners know key sections provoked discussion
  • A chat section to encourage discussion in each podcast (similar to NYTimes articles comments)
  • A discovery and recommendation engine so listeners can follow 'influencers' with good taste in podcasts

[Market Research]: I also looked at other interaction in different forms of media consumption, including Facebook Live Streaming, Medium highlighting and commenting, Twitter reactions, YouTube time-comment linkage, Quora blogging, and Instagram/ Spotify Discover.

After evaluating all of these ideas, I decided to choose 'Discovery' as the main feature to develop, since it most closely addressed the user needs I had uncovered in my user interviews. Discovery would provide additional value add in interacting with other users since it would save users time (in sifting through content), empower them to discover new content, and develop an emotional connection with the community (bonding through love of similar topics of interest).

Validation of Hypothesis: To test whether this feature would actually better the overall experience, I decided to implement an A/B test on the existence of this feature. Group A would have the current Stitcher app (without the new discovery feature) and we would test this against Group B (Discovery feature) and measure for metrics such as 1)how people engage with the feature and rate of return 3) what demographics are engaging.


4. Prototyping

To start thinking about possible layouts and functionality of the features to be tested in Group B, I first made the following assumptions and identified the following constraints in the interest of this exercise:

Assumptions:

  • Users are open to listening to podcasts as a valid form of content consumption
  • Users who utilize the facebook social login feature on the current Stitcher app are open to interacting with other users
  • Stitcher has the resources to mass A/B test
  • Stitcher has a large enough following for a statistically significant result in A/B testing

Constraints:

  • The current home screen in Stitcher has to stay the home screen because of paid content issues
  • We are developing this feature for when users are actively using the app (ex. their hands are not busy doing something else)
  • In this iteration we will not place accesibility as the first concern, though it is still of importance

I sketched out a number of ways a user could possibly discover new content: 1) through a social feed 2) through an influencer's profile.

5. Testing

The next step of this process would be to test and iterate on the design. Ideally, I would like to test the wireframes of this feature on a focus group to garner feedback on the functionality of the discovery interaction feature before releasing it to the public in the A/B test.

After getting feedback from the focus group, I would build out this feature and roll it out in an A/B test, gathering data on the metrics of success against the control group (no discovery feature) defined earlier:

  • Whether users are engaging with the discovery feature
  • If a user uses the discovery feature, does this increase usage of the app?
  • How are users engaging with the discovery feature? What demographics are engaging with the discovery feature?

If we are able to see a significant positive trend in increased usage of the app and increased engagement with the community, then I would suggest for Stitcher to roll out this feature to all users.


Acknowledgement of Shortcomings In This Exercise

As mentioned above, there were various assumptions and constraints taken into consideration when designing this test. However, this is quite an expensive testing method for a product feature rollout, and needs to take into account the business goals of such a feature (how much development/design resources need to be allocated towards this, whether there is a significant way to monetize this feature, and if this aligns with the long term goals of Stitcher). In addition, the prototypes designed above are purely draft one mockups, and have not gone through substantial user testing to determine effectiveness.
