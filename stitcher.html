Stitcher Product Challenge


The Challenge

Podcasting has gained popularity recently thanks to the rapid growth in smartphone use and the rise of wildly popular content like Serial. However, the technology that supports the creation and distribution of podcasts has evolved at a much slower pace compared to other mediums. Stitcher is one of the most widely used podcast platforms on the market right now.

Act as Stitcher's lead PM and assume listeners who interact with other users have a better overall experience. Determine:

  • The first feature to build to test this assumption and why
  • How this feature looks and behaves
  • Discuss tradeoffs, risks and metrics for success


Understanding the Challenge at Hand

To start off, we need to clarify the parameters of this hypothesis: "Listeners who interact with other listeners have a better overall experience". To break this hypothesis down, we need to clearly define "interact" and "better overall experience".

Interact

  • Liking / Reacting (Stitcher already has the 'favoriting' feature)
  • Curating (Stitcher already has the 'stitch together podcasts' feature)
  • Sharing & Discovering
  • Commenting & Discussing

Better overall experience

  • Increased satisfaction with the app (Could be measured via a rating popup)
  • Increased usage/retention of the app
  • Increased engagement with functionality of the app (Could be measured with increased time on screen)


Now that we have determined a number of possible paths these definitions could take, we need to delve deep into the needs of the user to thoroughly determine what feature would be most effective in testing this hypothesis.

3. Ideating

In the process of brainstorming new features to address these user needs and goals, here are a couple of ideas I came up with, including the following:

  • Integrations with wearables (headphones, apple watch etc. since their hands are busy) to bookmark/highlight/annotate their favorite sections in a podcast and share with other users
  • Real time transcribing of comments (while a podcast is playing) and having 'pings' to let fellow listeners know key sections provoked discussion
  • A chat section to encourage discussion in each podcast (similar to NYTimes articles comments)
  • A discovery and recommendation engine so listeners can follow 'influencers' with good taste in podcasts

[Market Research]: To fully understand the social aspect of this test, we also need to look at other interactions in different forms of media consumption, including Facebook Live Streaming, Medium highlighting and commenting, Twitter reactions, YouTube time-comment linkage, Quora blogging, and Instagram/ Spotify Discover, in addition to thoroughly investigating the current podcast apps out there.


After evaluating all of these ideas, I decided to choose 'DISCOVERY' as the main feature to develop, since it most closely addresses the user needs we see above in the user interviews. Discovery would provide additional value add in interacting with other users since it saves users time (in sifting through content), empowers them to discover new content, and helps them develop an emotional connection with the community (bonding through love of similar topics of interest). This feature would also address the gap in Stitcher's current product features and expand upon the two key advantages Stitcher already does well: Stitching (curating playlists) and Favoriting (signifiying what content they like).

Validation of Hypothesis: To test whether this feature would actually better the overall experience, we should implement an A/B test on the existence of this feature. Group A would have the current Stitcher app (without the new discovery feature) and we would test this against Group B (Discovery feature) and measure for metrics fleshed out in the testing section(5).


4. Prototyping

To start thinking about possible layouts and functionality of the features to be tested in Group B, we can make the following assumptions and identify the following constraints (in the interest of this exercise):

Assumptions:

  • Users are open to listening to podcasts as a valid form of content consumption
  • Users who utilize the facebook social login feature on the current Stitcher app are open to interacting with other users
  • We can utilize the Facebook API (since Stitcher already implemented a social login) and access a user's friends list and interests
  • Stitcher has the resources to mass A/B test
  • Stitcher has a large enough following for a statistically significant result in A/B testing

Constraints:

  • The current home screen in Stitcher has to stay the home screen because of paid content issues
  • We are developing this feature for when users are actively using the app (ex. their hands are not busy doing something else)
  • In this iteration we will not place accesibility as the first concern, though it is still of importance

5. Testing

The next step of this process would be to test and iterate on the design. Ideally, we should test the wireframes of this feature on a focus group to garner feedback on the functionality of the discovery interaction feature before releasing it to the public in the A/B test.

After getting feedback from the focus group, we will build out this feature and roll it out in an A/B test (Group A: control, Group B: discover), gathering data on the metrics of success defined below (based off of the 'better overall experience' definition):

  • The percentage of users in B group engaging with the feature
    • -from this metric we would be able to infer the amount of interest in the concept of discovery, and whether users would actually be interested in a social aspect to a podcast app
  • The return rate of users in B group in contrast to the return rate of users in A Group
    • -from this comparison we would be able to justify whether the existence of discovery would significantly contribute to the usage of the stitcher app and if it has enough 'stickiness' to draw a user back to the app
  • The time in seconds of B group users on the discovery screen
    • -from this metric we would be able to examine whether users are actively browsing through the suggestions offered, and if they are engaging with certain parts of the discovery screen more than others (demonstrates if they're interested in their friends' activities more than machine suggestions)
  • User pathing from the home screen and whether they discovered relevant content (can be determined by whether they listened to the podcasts from the discovery page in their entirety)
    • -from this metric we can determine if our recommendation engine is successful in surfacing relevant content to the user

To take this one step further, an in app survey about the satisfaction with the feature could also be conducted (once permission is obtained from the user), with three main questions

  • Has the discovery feature aided you in discovering relevant content to you? (Yes/No)
  • How often do you use the discovery feature? (On a scale of never to all the time)
  • The satisfaction with the discovery feature (open ended)

If we are able to see a significant positive trend in increased usage of the app and increased engagement with the community, then I would suggest for Stitcher to roll out this feature to all users.


Product Feature Prioritization

The following timeline balances the user goals and anticipated development time. It is important to note that since I do not have insight to the overarching long term product strategy, it is harder to immediately determine where this feature build fits into the product roadmap and determine possibilities of immediate monetization.

The first feature within Discover I hope to develop would be the curated playlists portion, as Stitcher already has a feature in place for "stitching together playlists" (less dev time). We would need to tap into this existing dataset and utilize Machine Learning to surface content that users would be interested in (essentially we would train a model to surface content of interest after defining characteristics of a user). After this subfeature is implemented, this would hopefully grow the user base, and therefore pave the way for the social aspect subfeatures such as 'reviewing podcasts', 'recommending', and 'real-time commenting'. Subsequently, network effects would take place and further grow the user base, and provide added value to the 'follow an influencer' subfeature.


Acknowledgement of Shortcomings In This Exercise

As mentioned above, there were various assumptions and constraints taken into consideration when designing this test. However, this is quite an expensive testing method for a product feature rollout, and we need to take into account the business goals of such a feature (how much development/design resources need to be allocated towards this, whether there is a significant way to monetize this feature, and if this aligns with the long term goals of Stitcher). In addition, the prototypes designed above are purely draft one mockups, and have not gone through substantial user testing to determine effectiveness.

As a PM, I would also be sure to thoroughly review all podcast apps on the market before coming to such a hypothesis that adding a social aspect is the determining factor in the success of our app.
