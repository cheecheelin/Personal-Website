Awechords Website Redesign



The Challenge

As a senior member of Awechords A Cappella, I was tasked with the redesign of our website to increase audition interest. I built this site from conception to finish at HackMIT 2016 in a 8 hr period, teaching myself the bootstrap framework and converting static wireframes into code.


The Process

While examining the legacy site (2015 version, coded by a fellow member of Awechords), I identified some issues with the flow of information. The primary goal of our website is to encourage new students to sign up for a timeslot for auditions and to give them a brief introduction as to what our group is about. This site is also meant to be a hub of all the online presences of Awechords.

The legacy site had a lot of information, but was not conducive in guiding the user through signing up for auditions. (I've included a full screenshot below.) I thus sketched out a flow that could be condensed into a one-pager, only providing vital information for the critical decision making points in the user journey.

The main three critical portions included:

  • about us: who we are as a group
  • members: the people in the group
  • join us: information on how to join

Having identified the three main areas of information, I wireframed the site in Adobe Xd and then pushed to code.
